    HEALTH SYSTEM SCIENCE?

    Health Systems Sciences (HSS) is the study that examines components of a health system to improve how healthcare is delivered and managed. Many people associate it primarily with the quality of healthcare services rendered to individual patients. In reality, that is just the visible tip of the iceberg in health systems science. HSS addresses many underlying interrelated factors that have contributed to the overall efficiency and effectiveness of healthcare services rendered to individual patients. These factors include healthcare policy, systems thinking, and population health management.

    As a discipline, Health Systems Science (HSS) focuses on interrelated disciplines: health system sciences, public health, and bioethics and health law. It aims to equip students with skills in analyzing how the healthcare system is organized, delivered, and can be improved, which are crucial skills needed in modern society. It takes a systems-thinking approach, which emphasizes understanding the complex interactions within healthcare settings. It explicates the importance of collaboration among healthcare professionals and developing strategies to enhance patients' experience in healthcare at an affordable cost for everyone.
